    Historical Problem Space (HPS) Framework

    The HPS framework will be beneficial for my unessay as it will allow to me to look at it from another perspective. I feel that this will help me to plan out what the purpose of my unessay is and how it will function. I like to belive that this framework helps me to gain a general picture of the elements behind a game. It allows a creator to analyze what is included in their games and see how all the elements work together to create the aesthetic of the game. 

    I like how the HPS helps to plan everything out. I feel that if a game creator uses this it allows them to see their game not only as that but in a way, as a method of teaching. In a historical context, I feel that it allows both the player and creator to look at the general context of a historical event and then realize the contents of the event. The HPS framework allows someone to realize why a historical event happened, they can analyze the people in the event as well as what happened and how this influenced other events. 

    In a historical context, I feel that it allows people to gain a further understanding of a situation and what happened. However, I do think that there would be some changes. I think that in a historical context it wouldn't be a "gameworld" but maybe more like a setting or place and instead of agents it would be something more like who was involved. In any way, I think that it helps to further study a historical event. 

    However, in class, even though we collectively tried to fill out this framework for the game Nothing Besides Remains, we came up with many different answers. This stood out to me. It made me realize that no matter how a game is produced, everyone will have their own personal opinion or view on it. The same can be said about a historical moment. While some historians may think that X happened at Z time, others may disagree and think that X happened at B time. And yet, that's what makes this framework fun.

        Well...

        The guide explained the history of Guatavita while we climed to the top of the lake. When we reached the very top, he told us that lake used be full to the very top of where we were standing. He mentioned that there was a legend of gold and riches to be found at the bottom of the lake and sadly, the Spaniards who were in Colombia around the 1500s wanted to find the gold. 

        In the second picture above, you can see where the Spanish started digging in attempt to drain the lake in order to lower the water level. From what I remember from what the guide told us, the Spanish found some gold but not as much as they were expecting. However, they started to see how this was affecting the environment of the forest and thankfully stopped digging. Sadly, the water level of the lake dropped drastically but the legend of gold at the bottom remains. 

        However, I went a few months ago, I do not remember all the details teh guide told us. I wanted to take this opportunity to not only learn more about my culture but also to be able to share part of Colombia's history with others as well. 

        So what format will my unessay be? I was thinking of a simulation game, possibly usuing Twine. I was thinking of making a game where you go back in time and become a Spaniard who is looking for gold. You converse with other Spaniards who discuss the plans of draining the lake in search for gold. But before that you are in the point of view of one of the Muisca people hearing about the legend of the lake at a gathering as this will provide context of the game. Overall, I was thinking the game go back and forth between the Spanish and Muisca POV to express what was going on during this time. I was thinking that towrds the end of the game, the player would go forward in time as of they are taking the same tour that I did and are hearing about the modern day thoughts about what happened at Lake Guatavita in the 1500s.
